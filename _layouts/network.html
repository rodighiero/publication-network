{{ page.heading | default: "Publication Network" }}

A live example of what you'll build this week — the publications of Dario Rodighiero, laid out by the similarity of their titles and abstracts. Hover a node to read its title; click to select it and reveal its closest matches.

Each node is one publication, placed near the others it most resembles. Similarity is computed offline from the title and abstract of every paper.

How the network is built

  1. Each file in _publications/ is plain Markdown — its first # heading is the title — and the whole document is used as-is.
  2. Each abstract's language is detected automatically; non-English ones — here Italian and French — are machine-translated to English with Helsinki-NLP/opus-mt-{lang}-en and cached on disk.
  3. Each document is embedded as a 768-dimensional vector by the BAAI/bge-base-en-v1.5 model.
  4. Pairwise cosine similarity yields a dense matrix over every pair of papers.
  5. An edge is drawn from each paper to its single strongest neighbour, unless even that best score falls below 0.70, in which case the paper stays unconnected.
  6. A D3 force simulation lays out the graph; the arrangement is pre-computed offline so it loads already settled.
